Summary

Timber & Torah is an immersive retreat series, blending traditional Jewish craftsmanship, spiritual practice, and land-based learning. Over the course of three distinct retreats—held in spring, late summer, and late fall—participants will use natural materials and traditional hand tools to construct a strawbale cabin from start to finish.

This initiative reconnects young Jews with ancestral skills and embodied Judaism. Through hands-on woodworking, Jewish ritual, and environmental stewardship, participants will gain confidence in heritage trades while fostering a tight-knit, purpose-driven community.

This retreat series is for those seeking meaningful, hands-on engagement with Jewish heritage—through building, learning, and creating together. More details coming soon!

Summary

Under the skillful guidance of writer, Haley Down, you’ll become a poet for the weekend. Even, and maybe especially, if you’ve never understood the point of poetry.

Together we will explore poetry as a tool for meaning-making, rather than solely a written form. What attracts people to this form of expression? What makes someone sit down to write a poem?

The work we do will be both personal and communal. We will walk, share meals, discuss meaning, read and write poetry. We will learn how moments of normalcy, transformed through poetry, help us create more time, more space for beauty and significance in our lives.

Things to note

Parts of this retreat will take place outside regardless of weather. Please be prepared to be outside in cold and snowy conditions.

Bring your own writing implements. The writing workshops will involve sharing drafts so hardcopy paper and pens is preferable to electronic devices but both can be accommodated.

One of our writing workshops will be inspired by a recipe that is special to you. Come prepared to discuss and contemplate this recipe.
